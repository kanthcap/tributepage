A brief look into Stan Lee's life:

  • 1922 - Stan Lee was born in Manhattan, New York
  • 1939 - Lee would join his uncle, Robbie Solomon, and become an assistant at the new Timely Comics division of pulp magazine. Timely Comics would then become the famous Marvel Comics.
  • 1941 - Lee makes his debut with a text filler in Captain America Comics #3 "Captain America Foils the Traitor's Revenge"
  • 1941 - Lee advances from writing text filler to writing actual comics and co-creates his first superhero, the Destroyer
  • 1961 - Working together with Jack Kirby, the two created their first superheros together: The Fantastic Four. They would then go on to create the Hulk, Thor, Iron Man, and the X-men.
  • 1972 - Lee stopped writing for hit monthly comic books and took on the role of publisher.
  • 1981 - He would then move to California to develop Marvel's TV and movie properties.
  • 2008 - Lee was awarded the 2008 National Arts Medal for pioneering work recreating American comic books
  • 2010 - The Stan Lee Foundation was founded to focus on literacy, education, and the arts. Its goals were to improve access to literary resources and promote diversity, national literacy, culture and the arts
  • 2018 - Lee passed away on November 12, 2018 but his legacy continues to live on in the stories he told
